FINAL: Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting improves arithmetic reasoning performance by encouraging large language models to generate intermediate reasoning steps before arriving at a final answer. Instead of attempting to map a question directly to an output, the model decomposes complex, multi-step problems into a series of logical, sequential sub-tasks.

This approach enhances performance through several key mechanisms:

*   **Decomposition of Complexity:** By breaking down a multi-step arithmetic problem into smaller, manageable parts, the model reduces the cognitive load required for each individual step, making it less likely to make errors in calculation or logic.
*   **Increased Transparency and Debugging:** The generation of intermediate steps provides a "trace" of the model's reasoning process. This allows for better interpretability, as the steps can be inspected to identify exactly where a potential error occurred.
*   **Improved Allocation of Computation:** CoT effectively allows the model to allocate more "compute" to the reasoning process by generating more tokens. This extra space for computation enables the model to maintain state and perform sequential operations that are difficult to execute in a single-step generation.

In essence, CoT transforms a challenging "black-box" inference task into a structured, step-by-step derivation, which significantly increases the accuracy of models on tasks requiring logical planning and arithmetic precision.
