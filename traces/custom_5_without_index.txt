FINAL: The primary difference between ReAct (Reason + Act) methodology and standard Prompt Engineering lies in how they manage the model's cognitive process and interaction with external environments.

*   **Standard Prompt Engineering** typically relies on static instructions or few-shot examples to guide the model toward a desired output. It often focuses on the "what" (the final answer) or uses techniques like Chain-of-Thought (CoT) to encourage internal reasoning, but the model remains largely isolated from external tools or real-time feedback during the generation process.
*   **ReAct Methodology** explicitly instructs the model to interleave reasoning traces with action steps. It forces the model to break down complex tasks into a sequence of "Thought," "Action," and "Observation" cycles. By generating a thought, performing an action (such as a search or API call), and then incorporating the observation from that action into its next thought, the model can dynamically adjust its strategy, correct errors, and gather necessary information that was not present in its initial training data.

In summary, while standard prompt engineering focuses on refining the model's internal logic or output format, ReAct transforms the model into an agent that actively probes its environment to solve complex, multi-step problems.
