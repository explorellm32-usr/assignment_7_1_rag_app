FINAL: Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting improves performance in arithmetic reasoning tasks by decomposing complex, multi-step problems into a series of manageable intermediate reasoning steps. Instead of forcing the model to map an input directly to an output, CoT encourages the model to generate a "chain of thought"—a sequence of logical steps that mirror the human problem-solving process.

This mechanism provides several key advantages:
*   **Decomposition:** It breaks down a difficult arithmetic problem into smaller, sequential sub-problems, which reduces the cognitive load on the model for each individual step.
*   **Interpretability and Debugging:** By explicitly generating intermediate steps, the model's reasoning process becomes transparent, allowing users to identify exactly where a potential error in logic or calculation occurred.
*   **Allocation of Computation:** It allows the model to dedicate more computational resources (in the form of generated tokens) to the reasoning process before arriving at the final answer, effectively increasing the "thinking time" spent on the problem.

In essence, CoT shifts the model's behavior from a single-step prediction to a structured, iterative process, which significantly increases accuracy on tasks that require logical planning and sequential calculation.
