FINAL: According to the "Attention Is All You Need" paper, the three main technical differences between self-attention and recurrent layers are:

1.  **Computational Complexity per Layer:** Self-attention layers have a computational complexity of $O(n^2 \cdot d)$ per layer, where $n$ is the sequence length and $d$ is the representation dimension. In contrast, recurrent layers have a complexity of $O(n \cdot d^2)$.
2.  **Parallelization:** Self-attention layers allow for total parallelization of computations across all positions in a sequence, whereas recurrent layers require sequential computation, which limits parallelization.
3.  **Path Length for Long-Range Dependencies:** Self-attention layers provide a constant path length of $O(1)$ for learning dependencies between any two positions in a sequence. Recurrent layers require a path length of $O(n)$ to connect two positions, making it more difficult to learn long-range dependencies.
